# Principles

These are the foundations the book builds on before the catalog.

## OOP foundations
- **Abstraction** — model only what matters in context.
- **Encapsulation** — expose a limited interface, hide internals.
- **Inheritance** — reuse behavior through extension.
- **Polymorphism** — work through shared contracts.

## Design principles highlighted in the book
- **Encapsulate what varies**
- **Program to an interface, not an implementation**
- **Favor composition over inheritance**

## SOLID lens
- **Single Responsibility Principle**
- **Open/Closed Principle**
- **Liskov Substitution Principle**
- **Interface Segregation Principle**
- **Dependency Inversion Principle**

## Review heuristic
Before naming a pattern, ask:
1. What is actually changing?
2. What must stay stable?
3. Is the pain in creation, structure, or behavior?
4. Does the code need a pattern, or just a simpler refactor?
